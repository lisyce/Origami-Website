About Origami

What Exactly Will You Learn Here?

Origami is the traditional Japanese art of folding paper. There are many takes on this craft. Some versions allow cutting and gluing. I prefer to stick to the traditional way- no cutting, gluing, or any other thing you can do to the paper except fold it.

About this site

Where was the idea from?

I got the idea to launch a website from scratch after taking an online course. Everything you see here was made without any special software. I hand-coded it. I was also tired of origami diagrams on sites, so I fixed that problem with my first large coding project- this site.

Supplies

What Does it Take to Make Origami?

It doesn't take much to make an origami figure. All you need is a square of paper. You can buy specialist origami paper at most local craft stores. In the image to the left, I have some 6 by 6 inch paper and some 3 by 3 inch paper.

Storage

What Should you do With Finished Origami & Extra Paper?

I like to put my 6 by 6 inch origami paper into quart-sized Ziploc bags. That way, it doesn't get bent if you have enough in there, and you don't have to worry about spilling your papers everywhere. As for finished origami models, I simply display them on a shelf in my room.
